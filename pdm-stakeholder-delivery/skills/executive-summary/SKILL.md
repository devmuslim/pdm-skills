# Skill: executive-summary

## Purpose
Generate a concise, one-page executive summary of delivery health — suitable for C-suite, board updates, or steering committee packets.

## When to Use
- Monthly business review
- Steering committee preparation
- When a senior leader asks "how are we doing?"

## Framework: Executive Delivery Summary

### Principle
Executives need signal, not noise. Every sentence must earn its place.
Format: What, So What, Now What.

### Step 1 — Gather Inputs
Ask for:
- Release or programme name
- Target date and current forecast
- Budget status (if relevant)
- Top 1–2 risks
- Any decisions needed from leadership

### Step 2 — One-Page Summary Format

---
**[Release / Programme Name] — Delivery Summary**
*As of [Date] | Prepared by [Name]*

**Overall Status**: 🟢 On Track / 🟡 At Risk / 🔴 Off Track

| | Planned | Forecast | Variance |
|--|---------|----------|---------|
| **Release Date** | [date] | [date] | [+/- days] |
| **Scope** | 100% | [X]% | [-Y items deferred] |
| **Budget** | $X | $Y | [+/- Z%] |

---

**Progress This Period**
- [2–3 bullet points: what was completed]

**Risks & Issues**
| Risk/Issue | Impact | Action | Owner |
|-----------|--------|--------|-------|
| [Top risk] | High | [Mitigation] | [Name] |

**Decisions Needed**
- [Explicit ask 1 — by when]
- [Explicit ask 2 — by when]

**Next Milestone**
[Milestone name] — [Date] — [Owner]

---

### Step 3 — Tone Rules for Executive Writing
- Lead with status (don't bury it)
- Use numbers, not adjectives ("3 weeks late" not "significantly delayed")
- Name owners explicitly
- Make asks specific ("Approve budget increase of $50K by Friday")
- No jargon (no "sprint velocity", "P1 bugs", "CI/CD")

## Output Format
1. Completed one-page executive summary
2. Talking points for verbal delivery (3 bullets)
3. Suggested next command: `/steering-deck` or `/status-update`
