# Skill: delay-communication

## Purpose
Craft professional, clear, and constructive communication when a delivery is delayed. Protects stakeholder trust while presenting a credible recovery plan.

## When to Use
- Release date needs to move
- Sprint scope will not be delivered as planned
- External dependency causes timeline slip
- Scope changes force a delay

## Framework: Delay Communication

### Principle: The 4 C's
Every delay communication must cover:
1. **Clarity** — What exactly is delayed, by how much
2. **Cause** — Why it happened (factual, no blame)
3. **Consequence** — What the impact is on downstream plans
4. **Correction** — What the recovery plan is

### Step 1 — Gather Information
Ask for:
- What was the original commitment (date, scope)?
- What is the new expected date or reduced scope?
- Root cause of the delay
- What has been done to recover?
- Who is the audience (team, stakeholders, executives)?

### Step 2 — Draft the Communication

**Subject**: Delivery Update: [Feature/Release Name] — Timeline Adjustment

**Opening** (1 sentence):
State the situation directly and without softening language.
> "The [Release Name] delivery has been revised from [original date] to [new date]."

**Context** (2–3 sentences):
Explain the cause factually.
> "The delay was caused by [root cause]. This was identified on [date] when [event/signal]."

**Impact** (1–2 sentences):
State downstream consequences clearly.
> "This affects [dependent teams/features/commitments]. [Downstream item] will now [move/adjust by X]."

**Recovery Plan** (bullet list):
- What actions are being taken
- By whom
- By when
- What will be delivered by the revised date

**What We Need** (if anything):
State explicit asks: decisions, approvals, resource support.

**Closing**:
One line expressing ownership and commitment.

### Step 3 — Tone Calibration
Adjust based on audience:
- **Team**: Direct, action-focused, collaborative
- **Stakeholders**: Professional, factual, solution-oriented
- **Executive**: Brief, impact-first, decision-ready

## Output Format
1. Ready-to-send communication (email or message format)
2. Talking points for verbal delivery
3. Suggested next command: `/status-update` or `/steering-deck`
