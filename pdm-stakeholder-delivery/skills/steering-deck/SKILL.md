# Skill: steering-deck

## Purpose
Build a concise, structured steering committee deck that drives decisions — not just updates.

## When to Use
- Monthly or quarterly steering committee meetings
- Go/No-Go decision meetings
- Major scope or timeline change requiring approval

## Framework: Steering Committee Deck

### Principle
A steering deck is not a status report. It is a decision-support document.
Every slide should answer: "What do you need from us?"

### Slide Structure (6 Slides Max)

---

**Slide 1 — Executive Dashboard**
- Programme/Release name
- Overall RAG status
- Date vs forecast
- Budget vs forecast
- Top risk in one line

---

**Slide 2 — Progress Since Last Meeting**
- What was committed → what was delivered
- Key milestones achieved
- Keep to 4–5 bullet points

---

**Slide 3 — Risks & Issues**
Table format:
| Risk/Issue | Status | Impact | Mitigation | Owner |
|-----------|--------|--------|-----------|-------|
Maximum 3–4 rows. If more exist, say "Full risk register available on request."

---

**Slide 4 — Decisions Required**
This is the most important slide.
Format each decision as:
- **Decision**: [What needs to be decided]
- **Options**: [Option A vs Option B]
- **Recommendation**: [Your recommendation]
- **Deadline**: [Date decision is needed]
- **Impact if delayed**: [What happens if no decision by then]

---

**Slide 5 — Next Period Plan**
- What will be completed before next steering meeting
- Key milestones and owners
- Any dependencies on steering committee

---

**Slide 6 — Appendix (Optional)**
- Detailed risk register
- Full milestone plan
- Team capacity summary

---

### Design Principles
- One message per slide
- Max 5 bullet points per slide
- Always include the date
- RAG status visible on every slide (header or footer)
- No walls of text

## Output Format
1. Slide-by-slide content (ready to paste into PowerPoint / Google Slides)
2. Speaker notes for each slide (2–3 sentences)
3. Suggested next command: `/decision-log` or `/status-update`
