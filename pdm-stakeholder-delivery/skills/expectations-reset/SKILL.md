# Skill: expectations-reset

## Purpose
Facilitate a structured reset of stakeholder expectations after a scope change, timeline slip, or delivery failure — without destroying trust.

## When to Use
- Release date has moved by more than 2 weeks
- Significant scope has been cut
- A committed feature will not be delivered as promised

## Framework: Expectations Reset

### Step 1 — Acknowledge Before Explaining
Never lead with reasons. Lead with acknowledgment.
> "I want to be transparent with you about a change to our delivery plan."

### Step 2 — State What Changed
Be precise:
- What was committed → what is now the reality
- Use specific numbers and dates, not vague language

### Step 3 — Explain Root Cause (Brief)
One or two sentences. No blame, no excuses.
> "This was caused by [X]. We identified it on [date]."

### Step 4 — New Commitment
State the new date or scope with confidence.
> "We are now committing to [new date / new scope]. This is a reliable estimate, not a best case."

### Step 5 — What We're Doing Differently
Show that the same problem won't happen again:
- Process change
- Risk mitigation
- Monitoring improvement

### Step 6 — Invite Questions
Close by inviting dialogue, not just passive acceptance.

## Output Format
1. Conversation script (verbal or written)
2. FAQ: anticipated questions with prepared answers
3. Suggested next command: `/status-update` or `/steering-deck`
