# Skill: stakeholder-pulse

## Purpose
Design and interpret a quick stakeholder pulse survey to measure confidence, satisfaction, and trust in the delivery team — and identify issues before they escalate.

## When to Use
- After a difficult delivery or delay
- Monthly as a health check
- Before a major release or steering committee

## Framework: Stakeholder Pulse Survey

### Step 1 — Survey Design (5 Questions Max)

**Question 1 — Overall Confidence** (1–5 scale)
> "How confident are you that the [release/programme] will deliver its committed outcomes?"
1 = Not confident at all → 5 = Very confident

**Question 2 — Communication Quality** (1–5 scale)
> "How satisfied are you with the frequency and clarity of delivery updates?"
1 = Very dissatisfied → 5 = Very satisfied

**Question 3 — Issue Response** (1–5 scale)
> "When issues arise, how effectively does the delivery team respond?"
1 = Very ineffectively → 5 = Very effectively

**Question 4 — Open Text — What's Working**
> "What is the delivery team doing well?"

**Question 5 — Open Text — Improvement**
> "What is one thing the delivery team could do better?"

### Step 2 — Distribution
- Keep it anonymous for honest feedback
- Share via email or Confluence — 2-minute completion time
- Send on a consistent cadence (e.g., last Friday of month)

### Step 3 — Interpret Results

| Average Score | Interpretation | Action |
|--------------|----------------|--------|
| 4.5–5.0 | 🟢 Excellent | Maintain and share success |
| 3.5–4.4 | 🟡 Good | Address specific concerns |
| 2.5–3.4 | 🟠 Needs attention | Stakeholder 1:1s recommended |
| < 2.5 | 🔴 Critical | Immediate recovery action |

### Step 4 — Response Template
After receiving results, send a short "You said, we heard, we'll do" response:

> "Thank you for your feedback. Here's what we heard and what we're doing:
> - You said: [theme from open text]
> - We heard: [interpretation]
> - We'll do: [specific action]"

## Output Format
1. Survey (ready to send)
2. Interpretation guide
3. Response template
4. Suggested next command: `/expectations-reset` or `/status-update`
