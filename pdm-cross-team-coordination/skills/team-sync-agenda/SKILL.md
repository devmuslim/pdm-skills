# Skill: team-sync-agenda

## Purpose
Prepare a focused, time-boxed agenda for multi-team sync meetings that drives decisions and actions — not just status updates.

## When to Use
- Weekly cross-team delivery sync
- Multi-team dependency review
- Release coordination meeting

## Framework: Sync Meeting Agenda

### Principle
Every sync meeting must end with decisions made and actions assigned. No decisions = no need for the meeting.

### Standard Agenda (45 Minutes)

**0–5 min — Check-in and Scope**
- Confirm attendees and quorum
- State the one goal of this meeting

**5–20 min — Status Round-Robin (3 min per team)**
Each team answers only:
1. What did we complete since last sync?
2. What's at risk?
3. What do we need from another team?

**20–35 min — Dependency Review**
- Review open dependencies from the dependency map
- Flag anything past due or newly at risk
- Assign owners and dates for unresolved items

**35–42 min — Decisions and Blockers**
- List decisions that need to be made in this meeting
- Make them (or explicitly escalate with owner and date)

**42–45 min — Actions and Close**
- Capture all actions: WHO does WHAT by WHEN
- Confirm next sync date

### Meeting Rules
- No status that can be read async — only what needs discussion
- Decisions must be made by someone present
- Actions must have a single owner and a date

## Output Format
1. Meeting agenda (ready to share in calendar invite)
2. Action log template
3. Suggested next command: `/integration-plan` or `/dependency-map`
