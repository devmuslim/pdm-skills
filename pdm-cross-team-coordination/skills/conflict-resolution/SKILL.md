# Skill: conflict-resolution

## Purpose
Facilitate structured resolution of cross-team priority conflicts using a neutral, data-driven framework. Helps PDMs broker agreement without escalating to leadership unnecessarily.

## When to Use
- Two teams disagree on priority or timeline
- Resource allocation dispute
- Dependency dispute (Team A blocking Team B)
- Disagreement on scope ownership

## Framework: Structured Conflict Resolution

### Principle
> "Separate positions from interests. Teams argue over positions ('We need this in Sprint 15') but share interests ('We both want the release to succeed'). Find the shared interest."

### Step 1 — Understand the Conflict
Ask:
- What are the two (or more) positions?
- Who are the stakeholders on each side?
- What is the business impact of each position?
- Is there a deadline making this urgent?

### Step 2 — Clarify Interests Behind Positions

| Team | Position (What they say) | Interest (Why they want it) |
|------|--------------------------|------------------------------|
| Team A | Need feature X in Sprint 15 | Customer demo on March 10 |
| Team B | Can't deliver X until Sprint 17 | Current focus on Platform stability |

### Step 3 — Impact Assessment

For each option, assess:
- Business impact if Team A gets their way
- Business impact if Team B gets their way
- Cost of delay
- Risk of compromise or partial delivery

### Step 4 — Generate Options
Brainstorm 3–4 resolution options:
1. **Team A's position** — Full priority, Team B adjusts
2. **Team B's position** — Delay, Team A adjusts timelines
3. **Compromise** — Partial delivery (MVP) by Sprint 15, full by Sprint 17
4. **Trade-off** — Team A gets X, Team B gets priority on Y next cycle

### Step 5 — Decision Framework

Score each option:
| Option | Business Value | Feasibility | Risk | Relationship Impact | Score |
|--------|---------------|-------------|------|---------------------|-------|
| Option 1 | 3 | 1 | 3 | 2 | 9 |
| Option 2 | 1 | 3 | 1 | 2 | 7 |
| Option 3 | 2 | 2 | 2 | 3 | 9 |

### Step 6 — Recommended Resolution
State the recommended option with rationale.
Draft a one-paragraph summary suitable for sharing with both teams.

### Step 7 — Escalation Trigger
If no resolution is possible at this level, define:
- What decision is needed from leadership
- By when
- What the consequences of delay are

## Output Format
1. Conflict summary (positions + interests)
2. Options table with scores
3. Recommended resolution with rationale
4. Stakeholder communication draft
5. Escalation brief (if needed)
6. Suggested next command: `/decision-log` or `/status-update`
