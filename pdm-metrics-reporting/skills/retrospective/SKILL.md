# Skill: retrospective

## Purpose
Facilitate a structured delivery retrospective — going beyond the sprint level to examine the full release or programme delivery, identify systemic patterns, and generate lasting improvements.

## When to Use
- After a major release
- End of a programme or quarter
- When recurring issues suggest systemic problems

## Framework: Delivery Retrospective

### Principle
A retro is only valuable if it produces specific, owned, time-bound actions. Without those, it's just venting.

### Format: 4Ls (Liked, Learned, Lacked, Longed For)

**Liked** — What went well that we should keep doing?
**Learned** — What did we discover that we didn't know before?
**Lacked** — What was missing that hurt our delivery?
**Longed For** — What do we wish we had?

### Session Structure (60–90 Minutes)

**5 min — Set the Stage**
- Retrospective Prime Directive: "Regardless of what we discover, we understand and truly believe that everyone did the best job they could, given what they knew at the time."
- No blame. Only systems.

**15 min — Individual Reflection**
Each person writes sticky notes (or Miro cards) for each of the 4Ls.

**20 min — Group and Theme**
Facilitator groups similar items. Team votes on top themes (dot voting).

**20 min — Discuss Top 3 Themes**
For each theme: What happened? Why? What would we do differently?

**15 min — Action Planning**
For each improvement: WHO does WHAT by WHEN.
Maximum 3 actions per retro — focus beats quantity.

**5 min — Close**
Rate the retro: Was it useful? (1–5) What would make it better?

### Action Table

| # | Improvement | Owner | Due Date | Status |
|---|------------|-------|----------|--------|
| 1 | Add dependency review to sprint planning | PDM | Next sprint | ⬜ Open |
| 2 | Create shared test environment for all teams | DevOps | 2 weeks | ⬜ Open |
| 3 | Run Three Amigos for all stories > 5 SP | PDM | Immediate | ⬜ Open |

### Follow-Up
Review retro actions at the start of the next retro.
If an action is not done: either do it or explicitly drop it.

## Output Format
1. Retro themes summary
2. Action table with owners and dates
3. Retro health score
4. Suggested next command: `/lessons-learned` or `/working-agreements`
